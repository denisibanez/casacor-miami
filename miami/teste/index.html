WELCOME
TO CASA COR
MIAMI

CASA COR, America’s greatest landscape, architecture and interior design event has just celebrated its 30th anniversary. CASA COR was founded in São Paulo and has expanded to the most important cities in Brazil, as well as to other countries.

This year, 23 CASA COR events will be produced, each focused on, inspiring and representing a local way of living. Over 600 thousand visitors will attend CASA COR in 2016, making it the second largest exposition event in its segment in the world.

In 2017, CASA COR will debut in Miami. With its amazing weather, natural beauty, Latin-infused multicultural, multilingual and multigenerational citizens, Miami is today the Capital of Latin America and an obvious, desirable place to host a CASA COR event. Real estate investments in Florida have risen rapidly and come from all parts of the globe. Seventy five percent of the 13 thousand condo units launched in Miami in the last 5 years have already been sold. Underdeveloped areas are being revitalized by private companies receiving breathtaking projects signed by the most selective team of starchitects. All of these recent initiatives, and many others, have transformed Miami into the new global destination for cultural events.

CASA COR Miami is proud to present the latest decoration trends, signed by the leading and rising architects, interior decorators and landscapers from around the world. CASA COR Miami 2017. Be there.

At CASA COR, stylish couples and groups of friends spend the day touring glamorous living rooms, dream suites and enviable kitchens. When hunger and fatigue strike, there are plenty of spots to relax and recharge one’s batteries, including restaurants, cafes and tranquil gardens. The work of some of the world’s most renowned landscapers, architects and interior designers, these retreats offer the public an elegant ambiance that reflects CASA COR’s dedication to home and hearth. “The message of elevated living, and the concept of home as the epicenter, is at the heart of CASA COR,” states Livia Pedreira, director of CASA COR Brazil.

CASA COR Brazil is part of The Abril Group, a media conglomerate formed by Abril Media Group (Abril Publishing and Abril Graphics) and DGB (Logistics and Distribution), is one of the biggest and most influent communication and distribution groups in Latin America.

Launched in 1987, CASA COR São Paulo today hosts over 100 thousand people every year (the exhibition lasts 50 days) and gave rise to other editions in Rio de Janeiro, Bahia, and 23 other cities. Each CASA COR franchise creates its own magazine with photography and text about its projects, available online at www.casacor.com.br.

CASA COR’s mission is to inspire people to transform their homes into temples of style and civility. It accomplishes that, as well as so much more: CASA COR discovers and introduces professional talent to the public and the market; it helps develop new technologies; and inspires new ways of living. Kitchens are integrated into the social area of the house, and bathrooms are treated as a relaxation zone. In the past three decades, a tendency towards multipurpose ambiances has been on the rise, according to the curator Roberto Dimberio. “In previous editions, there were living rooms that were made for hosting visitors and dining rooms that promised rare family gatherings. With time, the architects added more functions to the original ambience, reflecting the way that we live today,” states Dimberio, who cites the visitors’ experience as another factor in the success of the CASA COR formula.

“When visiting a museum, one looks at art behind the restricted yellow line. Not here. You can circulate through the rooms and around the furniture, feel the texture of the material and breathe the aroma in the air.” The CASA COR experience is enhanced by a diversity of solutions and products included in some 40 projects per edition. Imagine how many decorative objects, electronics, floorings, materials and bathroom amenities visitors have access to, all at once!

The CASA COR mission includes rediscovering historic city spots that have been forgotten over time. Once renovated, they become part of the city and residents’ daily routine. An example of this urban revitalization is the Jockey Club in São Paulo, which has hosted CASA COR since 2006. States CASA COR’s Livia Pedreira says: “Next year, our plan is to renovate and redevelop one of the buildings by creating a new roof and removing layers which overlapped the original project, of 1940.”

Last year in Rio de Janeiro, CASA COR was held in a block of two-story houses that had just gone through a six-year renovation. After the renovation, these houses – which become part of the city’s environmentally and culturally protected area – were allocated to a corporate rental program. The Rio show was responsible for the launch of this successful real estate initiative.

Beyond the green and yellow facade, CASA COR Peru has become known for the renovation of architectural monuments in the capital city and surrounding areas. Last year, it revitalized two palaces, the Matriz Square and the streets of Callao, a miserable port city close to Lima.

It is part of an extensive urbanization project which brings together the efforts of public authorities and private enterprises. To ensure that the community makes use of Callao’s historical center as a new cultural and leisure option, incentives are given to entrepreneurs to open new businesses in the area. It is because of CASA COR’s commitment to the community that many shops, art galleries and restaurants originally built for the exhibit space have remained vibrant after the event’s end, bringing new life to the region.
